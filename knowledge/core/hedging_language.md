# Hedging — Non-Negotiable Safety Layer

The Enneagram is a framework for self-understanding, not a diagnostic tool. We never:
- Claim anyone IS a specific type
- Diagnose mental health conditions
- Cross the line from personality into psychiatric categories

## Celebrity posts (CRITICAL)

Never: "Taylor Swift is a Type 4w3"
Always: "Taylor Swift **reads as** a Type 4w3"

### Approved hedging phrases (rotate)
- "reads as"
- "appears to exhibit traits consistent with"
- "publicly demonstrates behaviors often associated with"
- "based on interviews and public persona, may resonate with"
- "exhibits patterns consistent with"
- "commonly typed by the community as"

### Required disclaimer on every celebrity post
End with: *"Typing public figures is inherently speculative. This reflects publicly available material and common community consensus — not a professional assessment."*

## Mental health topics

When touching on: depression, anxiety, trauma, dissociation, addiction, self-harm, suicidal ideation

### Required framing
- "may correlate with" — NOT "causes"
- "some Fours report" — NOT "Fours suffer from"
- "research suggests a pattern of" — NOT "Fours have"

### Required disclaimer at the END
*"The Enneagram is a personality framework, not a medical diagnosis. If you're struggling, please reach out to a licensed professional."*

## Absolutes to avoid

| Don't write | Write instead |
|---|---|
| All Fours are... | Fours often... / Many Fours report... |
| Type 4s always feel... | For Fours, the experience is frequently... |
| Fours can't... | Fours may struggle to... |
| Fours never... | Fours tend to avoid... |

## Exception — when CAN we be direct?

When quoting an author directly, we use THEIR language (they may be more absolute). Frame it: "Naranjo goes as far as saying..." — this attributes the absolute to the author, not to us.
