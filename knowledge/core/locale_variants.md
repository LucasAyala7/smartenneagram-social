## Cultural touchstones

### US
- gym culture, therapy as lifestyle, college/grad school references
- "Thanksgiving", "spring break", "senior year"
- Therapy is mainstream conversation

### UK
- pub culture, flat-sharing in twenties, NHS references
- Self-deprecation > self-promotion
- Emotional restraint valued — lean slightly more reserved in tone
- "Mental health" more recently mainstreamed than in US

### CA
- Multiculturalism as norm; bilingual awareness (don't push)
- "Out west" vs "East Coast" divide
- Winter as cultural shared reference

### AU
- Dry humor, self-deprecating; don't over-earnest
- "Arvo" (afternoon), "servo" (gas station), "ute" (pickup) — use sparingly
- "Bush" (rural), "outback" — cliché-adjacent, use carefully
- Less therapy-saturated conversation than US

## What we DON'T do

- Never mock a locale
- Never fake an accent in text
- Never mix 2 locales in one post
- Never use explicit slang that ages badly (avoid TikTok-of-the-moment terms)

## Neutral (default)

When locale = `neutral`:
- Use US spelling (wider reach)
- Avoid regionalisms entirely
- Use generic references (coffee shop not Starbucks, subway not "the T")
- Prefer "morning / evening" over dayparts that vary
