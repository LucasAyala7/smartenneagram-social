# Type 4 — The Individualist (The Romantic)

## Essence
- **Center**: Heart (with 2 and 3)
- **Core fear**: having no identity or personal significance; being ordinary
- **Core desire**: to find themselves, their meaning, their unique significance
- **Passion**: Envy
- **Fixation**: Melancholy / self-absorption
- **Virtue**: Equanimity (emotional balance)
- **Holy Idea**: Holy Origin

## Core pattern
Fours focus on what's missing — in themselves, in life, in relationships. The envy is not "I want what they have" but "something essential is missing in me." They cultivate depth, uniqueness, and emotional intensity as counter-evidence to the fear of being ordinary.

## Observable behavior
- Romanticizes the unavailable; the absent becomes ideal
- Prefers intensity over contentment ("if it's not deep it's not real")
- Personal aesthetic as identity: clothing, music, apartment, name
- Feels more alive in longing than in having
- Self-absorbed cycle: internal weather becomes all-consuming
- Pushes away what's close; pulls toward what's distant
- Creative output as self-statement

## Wing colors
- **4w3**: more outward, ambitious, image-conscious; "the aristocrat"
- **4w5**: more withdrawn, philosophical, darker; "the bohemian"

## Instinct flavors
- **SP Fours**: suffer silently, stoic, masochistic endurance — Chestnut's countertype (least typical "Four")
- **SX Fours**: competitive, envious, expressive suffering, dramatic one-on-one intensity
- **SO Fours**: shame-prone, compares self publicly, sees self as victim of social dynamics

## Lines
- **Stress → 2**: clingy, over-giving to earn love, loses self in partner
- **Growth → 1**: disciplined action, grounded creative output, less "waiting for inspiration"

## Common misidentifications
- vs Type 2: Twos merge with others' needs; Fours differentiate and mourn the distance
- vs Type 6: both anxious, but Sixes scan for external threat; Fours scan for internal lack
- vs Type 5: both withdrawn, but Fives retreat from emotion; Fours marinate in it

## Content angles
- **Work**: the "I'm not like other professionals" identity; creative vs structured work fit
- **Relationships**: the push-pull; romanticizing ex-partners; the grass-is-greener loop
- **Stress**: depressive self-absorption; identity crisis
- **Growth**: grounded action over waiting for the "right mood"; ordinary as sacred
- **Self-discovery**: who are you when you're not suffering or longing?

## Key author associations
- Chestnut on the 3 Four subtypes (SP stoic / SX competitive / SO shame)
- Riso & Hudson on envy and the "imaginative melancholic"
- Naranjo on "frustration of need" and masochistic character
- Palmer on "the Tragic Romantic"

## What NOT to write
- ❌ "Fours are dramatic attention-seekers" — cruel, inaccurate
- ❌ "Fours are depressed" — conflating type with clinical diagnosis
- ❌ "Fours just need to be grateful" — moralizing that flattens the depth
- ❌ Glamorizing pain as the "Four aesthetic" — we aim for complexity, not sad-girl posting
