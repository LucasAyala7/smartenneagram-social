# Type 6 — The Loyalist (The Skeptic / The Questioner)

## Essence
- **Center**: Head (with 5 and 7)
- **Core fear**: being without support, guidance; being unable to survive alone
- **Core desire**: to have security, guidance, support
- **Passion**: Fear (the anxious scanning mind)
- **Fixation**: Doubt / cowardice
- **Virtue**: Courage
- **Holy Idea**: Holy Faith / Holy Strength

## Core pattern
Sixes scan the environment for threat. They build security through alliance, contingency plans, and testing others' trustworthiness. Two flavors: **phobic** (cautious, seeking authority) and **counterphobic** (rushes toward fear to conquer it). Same core pattern, opposite strategies.

## Observable behavior
- Plays devil's advocate; questions group consensus
- Loyalty once earned is fierce and long-lasting
- Asks "what could go wrong?" — not to be negative, but to be ready
- Tests authority figures (loved ones too) for trustworthiness
- Humor often deflective, self-deprecating
- Works well with structure and clear expectations
- Analysis paralysis when making big decisions

## Wing colors
- **6w5**: more withdrawn, intellectual, systematic skepticism
- **6w7**: more outgoing, humorous, restless anxiety

## Instinct flavors
- **SP Sixes**: warm, seek ally/friendship; phobic; anxious but approachable
- **SX Sixes**: counterphobic; intimidating, strong, denies fear by charging at it — Chestnut's countertype
- **SO Sixes**: duty-bound, rule-following, affiliates with systems (company, team, party, family)

## Lines
- **Stress → 3**: image-management, overwork, competitiveness, "prove I'm fine"
- **Growth → 9**: calm trust, less testing, acceptance of support

## Common misidentifications
- vs Type 1: Ones scan for error; Sixes scan for threat
- vs Type 4: both anxious, but Fours scan inward; Sixes outward
- vs Type 8 (for counterphobic 6): both forceful, but Eights dominate from strength; counterphobic Sixes from fear of fear

## Content angles
- **Work**: loyalty vs burnout; questioning authority vs being "difficult"
- **Relationships**: the trust test; counterphobic vs phobic attachment
- **Stress**: analysis paralysis; catastrophizing
- **Growth**: trusting inner authority instead of outsourcing
- **Self-discovery**: naming fear explicitly; acting despite it

## Key author associations
- Chestnut on the SX 6 counterphobic as countertype
- Riso & Hudson on "the security-oriented person"
- Naranjo on cowardice/persecutory-accusatory character
- Palmer on "the Trooper/Devil's Advocate"

## What NOT to write
- ❌ "Sixes are paranoid" — conflates personality with clinical delusion
- ❌ "Sixes just worry" — misses loyalty/courage/duty dimensions
- ❌ "Stop being so anxious!" — unhelpful; name the source instead
