# Type 2 — The Helper (The Giver)

## Essence
- **Center**: Heart (with 3 and 4)
- **Core fear**: being unworthy of love, unwanted
- **Core desire**: to be loved, needed, essential to others
- **Passion**: Pride (the disguised kind — "I don't need anything, but you need me")
- **Fixation**: Flattery / image of loveliness
- **Virtue**: Humility
- **Holy Idea**: Holy Will / Freedom

## Core pattern
Twos earn love by anticipating needs before they're spoken. The trap: their own needs are inadmissible, so they register only indirectly — as exhaustion, resentment, or "why doesn't anyone notice?" Pride is the refusal to need, masked as generosity.

## Observable behavior
- Remembers everyone's preferences, allergies, birthdays
- Repositions around the person they want to be close to
- Praise, gifts, small favors — currency of connection
- Difficulty asking directly for what they want
- Resentment flares when the helping isn't noticed or reciprocated
- Hyper-aware of others' moods; their own mood is invisible to them

## Wing colors
- **2w1**: more serious, dutiful, service-oriented, boundaried helping
- **2w3**: more ambitious, image-conscious, charming, can perform warmth

## Instinct flavors
- **SP Twos**: childlike, more privately needy, "take care of me so I can take care of you" — Chestnut's countertype
- **SX Twos**: seductive, fiery, intense one-on-one, claims the other
- **SO Twos**: ambitious helper, influences groups, "behind every important person"

## Lines
- **Stress → 8**: sudden directness, rage, "after all I did for you"
- **Growth → 4**: turns inward, finds own authentic feelings, owns own needs

## Common misidentifications
- vs Type 9: both accommodate, but Nines merge to avoid conflict; Twos actively pursue connection
- vs Type 3: both image-aware, but Threes chase achievement; Twos chase being needed
- vs Type 6: both relational, but Sixes scan for loyalty/threat; Twos scan for need

## Content angles
- **Work**: being the "glue" no one notices; burnout from invisible labor
- **Relationships**: the "I don't need anything" trap; codependency patterns
- **Stress**: the resentful helper; unacknowledged exhaustion
- **Growth**: naming needs out loud; self-care that isn't self-martyrdom
- **Self-discovery**: who are you when no one needs you?

## Key author associations
- Chestnut on the SP 2 as countertype
- Riso & Hudson on "the possessive intimate"
- Naranjo on pride as "inflation of self-image"
- Palmer on the Helper's identification with others' needs

## What NOT to write
- ❌ "Twos are manipulative" — reductive and cruel
- ❌ "Just set boundaries!" — the how is the whole journey
- ❌ "Twos are martyrs" — language to avoid outside Naranjo-direct-quote context
