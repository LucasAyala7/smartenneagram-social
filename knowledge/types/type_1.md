# Type 1 — The Reformer (The Perfectionist)

## Essence
- **Center**: Body/Gut (alongside 8 and 9)
- **Core fear**: being corrupt, defective, evil, imperfect
- **Core desire**: to be good, virtuous, right, with integrity
- **Passion**: Anger (specifically, resentment — anger pushed down because it's "not nice")
- **Fixation**: Resentment / perfectionism
- **Virtue**: Serenity
- **Holy Idea**: Holy Perfection (reality is already whole)

## Core pattern
The internal critic never turns off. Fours feel incomplete; Ones feel *incorrect*. They see the gap between "what is" and "what should be" and feel morally responsible for closing it — often before they've even permitted themselves rest.

## Observable behavior
- Double-checks work repeatedly
- Corrects small errors in others' speech/grammar without meaning to
- Guilt-response to fun, rest, pleasure
- "Should" is the most used verb in their internal monologue
- Body tension (jaw, shoulders) is chronic
- Angry at injustice in the world, but angrier at their own shortfall

## Wing colors
- **1w9**: quieter, more withdrawn, philosophical, detached perfectionism
- **1w2**: warmer, more interpersonal, advocacy-oriented, can tip into moralizing

## Instinct flavors
- **SP Ones**: anxiety-focused; worry, preparation, body-stiffness; the "model citizen" archetype
- **SX Ones**: the reformer; intense, zealous, jealous of the imperfect; most fiery — Naranjo's countertype
- **SO Ones**: rigid, teacher/role-model energy, socially "above reproach"

## Lines
- **Stress → 4**: slips into self-loathing, envy, moodiness, "nobody gets it right"
- **Growth → 7**: lightness, play, permission to want things for pleasure alone

## Common misidentifications
- vs Type 3: Ones optimize for "right", Threes optimize for "winning/image"
- vs Type 6: both anxious, but Sixes scan for danger; Ones scan for error
- vs Type 8: both intense about justice, but Eights channel anger outward; Ones push it inward first

## Content angles
- **Work**: perfectionism as productivity ceiling; delegation paralysis
- **Relationships**: critical loop, the "you'd love me more if I were better" ache
- **Stress**: resentment as mental loop; body tension
- **Growth**: permission, play, spontaneity
- **Self-discovery**: the inner critic; separating self-worth from performance

## Key author associations (for citations)
- Naranjo on "reactive-anti" structure
- Riso & Hudson on health levels for "the orderly person who becomes obsessive"
- Chestnut on the three 1 subtypes (SP Worry, SX Zeal, SO Rigidity)
- Wagner on the 1's "effort to be perfect"

## What NOT to write
- ❌ "Ones are uptight control freaks" — reductive
- ❌ "Just relax!" framing — Ones hate it and it flattens the depth
- ❌ "Type 1 = OCD" — conflates personality with psychiatric diagnosis
