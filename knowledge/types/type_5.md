# Type 5 — The Investigator (The Observer)

## Essence
- **Center**: Head (with 6 and 7)
- **Core fear**: being overwhelmed, depleted, incompetent, invaded
- **Core desire**: to be capable, self-sufficient, competent
- **Passion**: Avarice (hoarding — of time, energy, knowledge, privacy)
- **Fixation**: Stinginess / withholding
- **Virtue**: Non-attachment (generosity that flows without clinging)
- **Holy Idea**: Holy Omniscience / Holy Transparency

## Core pattern
Fives minimize needs to remain free. Resources (attention, energy, time, emotion) feel finite; giving drains the reserve. They retreat into mastery of a domain, observing life more than participating. Competence becomes a shield.

## Observable behavior
- Prefers fewer, deeper relationships
- Needs quiet recovery after social exposure
- Schedules mind like resources — won't "waste" energy
- Curiosity without requirement to share findings
- Dry, spare communication; precise word choice
- Discomfort with other people's emotional demands
- Private, sometimes to the point of secretive

## Wing colors
- **5w4**: more artistic, emotional, unusual; philosophical loner
- **5w6**: more analytical, loyal, system-oriented; problem-solver

## Instinct flavors
- **SP Fives**: fortresses; guarded, private, few but intense attachments
- **SX Fives**: drawn to one confidant, intense privacy + intense connection, romantic/idealizing — Chestnut's countertype
- **SO Fives**: expert / "the sage"; contributes knowledge to a group, more visible than SP

## Lines
- **Stress → 7**: scattered, impulsive escape, sudden externalizing
- **Growth → 8**: grounded action, body engagement, claiming space

## Common misidentifications
- vs Type 4: Fours marinate in emotion; Fives intellectualize away from it
- vs Type 6: Sixes scan for danger interpersonally; Fives scan for depletion
- vs Type 9: both withdrawn, but Nines blur; Fives sharpen

## Content angles
- **Work**: deep work vs social work; collaboration drain
- **Relationships**: intimacy as threat AND longing; the privacy paradox
- **Stress**: detachment becoming isolation; resource-hoarding dysfunction
- **Growth**: embodiment, presence, allowing need
- **Self-discovery**: what's behind the wall of competence?

## Key author associations
- Chestnut on the SX 5 (countertype — more romantic than expected)
- Riso & Hudson on "the expert" and detachment
- Naranjo on "detachment from experience"
- Maitri on Holy Omniscience

## What NOT to write
- ❌ "Fives are antisocial" — inaccurate; they have deep relationships, fewer of them
- ❌ "Fives are cold" — flattens the rich inner life
- ❌ "Fives are introverts" — conflates type with introversion/extraversion axis
- ❌ "Just be more open!" — misses the resource-logic underneath
