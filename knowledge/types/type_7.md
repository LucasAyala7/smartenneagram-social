# Type 7 — The Enthusiast (The Epicure)

## Essence
- **Center**: Head (with 5 and 6)
- **Core fear**: being trapped in pain, limited, deprived
- **Core desire**: to be satisfied, fulfilled, free, enjoying life
- **Passion**: Gluttony (of experience, not just food — more options, more novelty)
- **Fixation**: Planning / anticipation
- **Virtue**: Sobriety (presence with what is)
- **Holy Idea**: Holy Work / Holy Wisdom / Holy Plan

## Core pattern
Sevens sublimate pain by redirecting the mind to what's next. The trap: anticipation becomes more pleasurable than arrival. FOMO is structural, not circumstantial. Options must remain open — commitment feels like loss of possibility.

## Observable behavior
- Multiple simultaneous projects, trips, ideas
- Reframes difficulty fast: "but actually, this is great because..."
- Talks quickly, associates wide
- Struggles to finish — the excitement was in starting
- Avoids negative emotion — changes subject, moves bodies, plans something
- Charming, charismatic, fun in groups
- Underneath: a sense that pain deferred is pain managed

## Wing colors
- **7w6**: more responsible, loyal, grounded enthusiasm; "the entertainer with depth"
- **7w8**: more aggressive, driven, materialistic; "the realist who wants more"

## Instinct flavors
- **SP Sevens**: "the family / alliance" — networked, practical, hedonistic in a groupy way
- **SX Sevens**: idealize, dreamy, romantic; projects onto partners/futures; least "flighty"-seeming
- **SO Sevens**: sacrifice gratification for social/meaningful work — Chestnut's countertype

## Lines
- **Stress → 1**: critical, rigid, resentful, "perfecting" the escape
- **Growth → 5**: depth, focus, commitment to one domain, real presence

## Common misidentifications
- vs Type 3: Threes optimize for winning; Sevens optimize for experience
- vs Type 8 (for 7w8): Eights dominate; Sevens escape
- vs Type 2: Twos merge; Sevens move on

## Content angles
- **Work**: starting > finishing; multi-tasking as avoidance
- **Relationships**: the charm vs commitment gap; grass-is-greener
- **Stress**: manic planning as pain-avoidance
- **Growth**: staying with the hard thing; sobriety not as abstinence but as presence
- **Self-discovery**: what pain are you outrunning?

## Key author associations
- Chestnut on SO 7 countertype (the sacrificing Seven)
- Riso & Hudson on gluttony and the "busy, fun-loving type"
- Naranjo on "flight" and revolutionary/planning fixation
- Palmer on "the Epicure"

## What NOT to write
- ❌ "Sevens are shallow" — misses the sophisticated pain-avoidance
- ❌ "Sevens are ADHD" — conflates type with clinical diagnosis
- ❌ Flattening Sevens into "fun party type" — their depth is there
