# Pillar D — Myth-busting / Comparison

## Purpose
Correct widespread misunderstandings. Establish authority by debunking. High share-rate because readers feel "finally, someone says it."

## Two sub-formats

### D1 — Myth-busting
"Myth: Fours are always depressed. Reality: Fours are melancholic-romantic, which is different."

### D2 — Comparison (type confusion)
"Are you a 2 or a 9? They look similar — here's the difference."

## Common myths to bust

### By type
- "Ones are control freaks" → inner critic is internal, not interpersonal
- "Twos are fake" → their help is real, what's hidden is their need
- "Threes are shallow" → missing the shape-shifting + hollow-feeling
- "Fours are drama queens" → the depth is real, the stereotype is reductive
- "Fives are antisocial" → few deep relationships, not none
- "Sixes are paranoid" → scanning ≠ clinical paranoia
- "Sevens are shallow" → pain-avoidance is sophisticated, not dumb
- "Eights are bullies" → protection of self/others is the core, not aggression
- "Nines are lazy" → sloth = self-forgetting, not physical laziness

### General
- "Enneagram is astrology" → astrology assigns by birth; Enneagram is psychological
- "Enneagram has no science" → validation in progress; Big Five correlations exist
- "Your type changes over time" → core type stable; expression changes
- "Higher numbers are better" → numbers are categorical, not ranked

## Comparison pairs (classic confusions)
- 2 vs 9: both accommodate; 2 actively gives, 9 passively merges
- 3 vs 1: both strive; 3 for image, 1 for correctness
- 4 vs 6: both anxious; 4 scans inward, 6 outward
- 5 vs 9: both withdraw; 5 sharpens, 9 blurs
- 6 (counterphobic) vs 8: both forceful; 6 from fear, 8 from strength
- 7 vs 3: both energetic; 7 seeks experience, 3 seeks achievement
- 1 vs 3: both high-functioning; 1 "right", 3 "win"

## Format
- M2 (Torn Paper / News) — provocative headline
- M6 (Split-Screen) — side-by-side comparison
- M3 (Carousel) — deeper myth-bust

## Hook patterns
- "Myth: [claim]. Here's the reality."
- "Stop saying [stereotype]. Here's what's actually happening."
- "Are you a [X] or a [Y]? The difference is [thing]."
- "The most common misreading of Type [N]"

## Mix for 30-day calendar
- 15% of IG content
- 15% of LinkedIn (myth-busting LOVES LinkedIn)

## Do
- State the myth clearly
- Offer the corrective with citation
- Be firm, not preachy
- Invite the reader to "tag someone who needs this"

## Don't
- Mock the myth-believer
- Be so clever the point is lost
- Skip the evidence (citation is non-negotiable here)
