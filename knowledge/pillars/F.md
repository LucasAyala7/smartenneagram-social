# Pillar F — Behind the Science

## Purpose
Establish SmartEnneagram as the evidence-backed, psychometric, serious player. Nobody else in consumer Enneagram does this.

## Primary channel
**LinkedIn** — 25% of LinkedIn content. HR pros, coaches, L&D leaders, clinicians: this pillar speaks to them.

## IG role
5% of IG content. Keeps the authority frame alive for general audience.

## Topics
- Big Five correlations (use `big5_correlation` in TypeMeta)
- Psychometric validation (ours in progress, others' published)
- Methodology (per-question insights, adaptive premium, dual AI synthesis)
- Research literature mapping
- Measurement theory (what "type" means — categorical vs dimensional)
- Comparing Enneagram to other frameworks (MBTI, Big Five, HEXACO) honestly

## Format preferences
- **M9 (Data Viz)** — the signature format for F
- **M3 (Carousel)** — methodology deep-dives
- **M2 (Torn Paper)** — research-study framing

## Tone
- Third-person where possible
- Academic but accessible ("The factor loadings suggest..." vs "So here's the thing...")
- Cite explicitly (paper title, year, author — like an academic post)
- Don't hide uncertainty — "preliminary data suggests", "ongoing validation shows"

## Hook patterns
- "Big Five data on Type 5: what the numbers show"
- "Is the Enneagram measurable? The current state of validation research."
- "Why we built dual AI synthesis: the methodology behind SmartEnneagram"
- "Per-question analysis vs summary reports: why it matters"

## The honest framing
- Enneagram's psychometric status is WEAKER than Big Five
- iEQ9 has published validation; we're building ours
- Many Enneagram claims lack empirical support
- Our position: "We're the consumer test most honest about this"

## Safety
- Cite real studies (only ones we actually have access to)
- Never overclaim ("proven", "scientifically proven", "clinically validated")
- Use appropriate language: "correlational", "preliminary", "face validity"

## Mix for 30-day calendar
- IG: 5% (maybe 1-2 posts/month)
- LinkedIn: 25% (1 post/week minimum)

## Do
- Bring the data
- Name the author + year of published studies
- Own the limitations
- Link to white paper / methodology page when appropriate

## Don't
- Inflate claims
- Imagine studies that don't exist
- Use jargon without defining
- Sound defensive about Enneagram's limits — own them as part of the brand honesty
