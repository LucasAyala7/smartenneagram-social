# Pillar C — Type in Context

## Purpose
Show how each type SHOWS UP in specific life domains. This is the "applied" pillar — readers recognize patterns in their actual life.

## Contexts (rotate these)
- **Work**: meetings, leadership, collaboration, productivity
- **Relationships**: dating, long-term, family, friendships
- **Stress**: under pressure, in conflict, burnout
- **Growth**: what helps this type heal/develop
- **Self-discovery**: inner journey, shadow work
- **Money**: spending, earning, saving patterns (less common, high-engagement)
- **Health / body**: sleep, exercise, eating patterns (careful — not medical)

## Format
- M3 (Carousel Deep-Dive) is the primary model
- Also: M1 (photo + headline), M8 (reel for energetic takes)

## Carousel structure (M3 specifically for C)
- Slide 1: "Type X at work" / "Type X in relationships"
- Slide 2-4: 3 specific patterns
- Slide 5: 1 failure mode
- Slide 6: 1 growth move
- Slide 7: Citation
- Slide 8: CTA

## Tone by context
- **Work/relationships**: warm, practical, actionable
- **Stress**: compassionate, not alarmist
- **Growth**: hopeful, specific ("not easier, but clearer")
- **Self-discovery**: reverent, deep

## Hook patterns
- "How Type [X] navigates [context]"
- "What [Type name]s do when [context trigger]"
- "[Type name]s at work: the pattern no one names"
- "The thing [Type name]s do in conflict"

## Mix for 30-day calendar
- 20% of IG content
- 20% of LinkedIn content (especially work context)
- Rotate contexts weekly

## Do
- Use specific scenarios
- Name the pattern crisply
- Connect theory author to applied behavior
- Offer ONE reframe, not 10

## Don't
- Stay abstract ("Fours are emotional" — USELESS)
- Treat all contexts the same
- Skip the citation (context pillar still needs anchoring)
- Give generic "growth tips" that don't honor the type's internal logic
