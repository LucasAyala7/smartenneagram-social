# Pillar B — Citation of the Day

## Purpose
Build the BRAND as the cited, academic, evidence-backed Enneagram. Run daily on IG as a series. This pillar IS our moat.

## Why this is strategic
No other consumer Enneagram account does this. We have 5,513 citations with author + year + page for 44% of them. Running 30 days of citation cards establishes credibility that takes competitors years to match.

## Format preference
- **M5 Citation Card** is the primary model
- Occasionally M3 carousel (one citation + deeper unpacking)

## Selection rules
- **Prioritize citations WITH page number** (our rare asset)
- Rotate across 9 authors AND 9 types (so no type or author monopolizes)
- Avoid citations already used in past 60 days (anti-repetition)

## Caption structure
- Hook = paraphrase the citation in contemporary words
- Insight = what the author meant, what they saw
- Citation block = the quote itself (even though it's on the image)
- Reflection = what this lands for the reader today
- CTA = "This is 1 of 5,513 citations our test uses. Find yours."

## Selection heuristics
- The quote should be self-contained (no prior context needed)
- 15-80 words ideal
- Preferably captures an INSIGHT, not a definition
- Mix famous authors (Naranjo, Riso & Hudson, Chestnut) with deeper cuts (Salmon, Webb, Wagner)

## Author rotation target
- Naranjo: 20%
- Riso & Hudson: 20%
- Chestnut: 15%
- Daniels & Price: 10%
- Palmer: 10%
- Maitri: 10%
- Salmon / Webb / Wagner: 15% combined

## Mix for 30-day calendar
- 15% of IG content on weekdays
- On LinkedIn: 25% (single slide posts, high-signal)

## Do
- Keep the image clean — the quote IS the art
- Always cite: author + work + year + page
- Paraphrase in caption, quote on image

## Don't
- Repeat the same citation within 60 days
- Use citations without page when one with page exists for that theme
- Invent page numbers
- Over-design the card (typography is enough)
