# M2 — Torn Paper / News Style

## When to use
- Pillar D (Myth-busting / Comparison)
- Pillar F (Behind the Science) — with research angle
- Works on IG Feed AND LinkedIn (editorial feel translates)
- Triggers: curiosity, provocation

## Visual formula
- Grainy torn newspaper / magazine clipping aesthetic
- Bold serif headline (magazine-style)
- Smaller italic subhead
- Tiny byline-style brand attribution bottom
- Sepia/muted palette

## Headline discipline
- Written as if it's a news pull-quote
- 6-12 words
- Patterns:
  - "*Study suggests: [surprising finding]*"
  - "*[Group] more likely than thought to [behavior]*"
  - "*Research from [source]: [claim]*"
- Must be DEFENSIBLE — if the post makes the headline clickable, the content must deliver

## Image prompt guidance
- Torn paper texture, visible grain
- Sepia or cool-gray palette
- Serif typography integrated into the composition
- Optional small photograph embedded (tiny, vintage feel)
- No AI-shiny vibe — intentional imperfection

## Copy-side strategy
- Caption starts with the "source" feel: "A study from [Author, Year] found..."
- Middle explains the finding
- Citation block anchors it
- End asks a reflection question

## Common mistakes
- ❌ Sensationalizing beyond the research — credibility is the brand
- ❌ Making up a study — only reference authors in our acervo
- ❌ Modern sans-serif — breaks the editorial illusion
- ❌ Over-photoshopped torn edges — should look real/analogue

## Hedging
- Headlines in this style MUST be accurate. A "Study suggests" that doesn't match the citation is a brand-damage risk.
