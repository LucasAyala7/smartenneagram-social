# M9 — Data Viz / Infographic

## When to use
- Pillar F (Behind the Science) — primary
- LinkedIn (strongest fit)
- IG Feed (secondary, still works with the educated audience)
- When the content is actually numbers / correlations / distributions

## Content sources (where to pull data)
- `big5_correlation` in TypeMeta (each type's Big Five pattern)
- `research_data` in TypeMeta
- Published Enneagram psychometric studies (cite the ones we already have in acervo)
- Distributions claimed in Chestnut's or Riso & Hudson's work (attribute carefully)

## Data Viz types we use
- **Radar chart** — Big Five profile of a type (5 axes)
- **Bar chart** — prevalence, score comparisons
- **Scatter / 2D map** — types arranged on 2 dimensions
- **Distribution curve** — score bell curves with cutoffs
- **Side-by-side comparison** — 2 types on the same metric

## Visual formula
- Max 2-3 brand colors (primary green + one accent)
- White or off-white background
- Sans-serif labels (Inter)
- Numbers large and readable
- Source citation at the bottom (small but visible)
- Title at the top, subtitle clarifying the measurement

## Honesty rules
- NEVER invent numbers
- If using indicative patterns (not measured), label clearly: "Illustrative pattern based on [Author, Year]"
- Always cite the source
- Error bars / confidence intervals when data exists
- Don't mislead with scale manipulation (y-axis starting at 0 unless there's a reason)

## Copy-side strategy
- Hook = the surprising data point ("Fives score 0.85 on Openness — the highest of any type")
- Body = interpret what it means in plain language
- Citation of author who made the claim
- Reflection = "Does your lived experience match?"
- CTA = "Our test's 115 per-question insights map to Big Five — link in bio"

## Common mistakes
- ❌ Made-up statistics
- ❌ Over-stylized charts (our brand is credibility, not decoration)
- ❌ Missing source citation
- ❌ Misleading y-axis
- ❌ "Scientific-feeling" design with no actual research behind it

## Why M9 is strategic
Competitors in Enneagram space NEVER post data viz. It signals that we're the serious, research-backed player. Running 2 M9 per month on LinkedIn builds the "Behind the Science" moat.
