# M4 — Interactive / Quiz

## When to use
- Pillar E (Interactive / Quiz) — primary
- Recruit-to-test moment
- IG Story with sticker OR IG Feed with visual choices
- Trigger: curiosity

## Format
- A question + 2-4 answer options
- Answer pattern implies a type
- End CTA: "Take the full test to confirm"

## Question design — high-converting patterns

### Pattern A — "Which quote resonates?"
Slide/post: "Pick the quote that hits the hardest:"
- A: "I cannot rest until it's right" — [hints Type 1]
- B: "I don't need anything, but you need me" — [hints Type 2]
- C: "Nobody really gets me" — [hints Type 4]

End caption: "Each quote is from our acervo — it hints at an Enneagram type. Which one were you? Comment the letter and we'll tell you more about yours."

### Pattern B — "Which scenario are you?"
"You get to a party and your default move is:"
- A: Scan the room for who's uncomfortable (Type 2)
- B: Find one interesting person and stay with them (Type 5)
- C: Entertain the group, shift mood upward (Type 7)
- D: Leave early, already tired (Type 9 or 5)

### Pattern C — "Complete the sentence"
"The thing I hate most is:"
- Being wrong (Type 1 / 3 / 6)
- Being unseen (Type 4 / 2)
- Being controlled (Type 8 / 7)
- Being overwhelmed (Type 5 / 9)

## Visual formula
- Bold minimalist design
- Large typography
- Options as cards (2x2 grid or vertical stack)
- Option letter A/B/C/D visible
- No decoration — the question IS the design

## Copy-side strategy
- Caption is shorter here (image IS the content)
- Hook = the question
- Body = reveal that each option hints at a type
- Soft CTA: "Comment which one you picked"
- Hashtags still 15-25

## Common mistakes
- ❌ Too many options (4 max)
- ❌ Options that clearly hint the type ("I'm a perfectionist" — gives it away)
- ❌ Questions that don't differentiate (must require different types to pick differently)
- ❌ Leaving the type-answer reveal absent from the caption

## Ethics
- Don't trick readers into thinking this IS their type. The quiz is a hook — the test is the answer.
