# M6 — Split-Screen Comparison

## When to use
- Pillar D (Myth-busting / Comparison)
- Type vs Type confusion ("Are you a 2 or a 9?")
- Healthy vs Unhealthy of same type
- Wing comparison (4w3 vs 4w5)
- Instinct variants (SP vs SX vs SO of one type — 3-panel variant)
- IG Feed + LinkedIn

## Format
- Two (or three) distinct visual zones
- Each zone has: title, color palette, 3-5 traits in bullets
- Thin divider line between zones
- Bottom: small caption clarifying "this is a simplification"

## Comparison patterns

### Type vs Type confusion
- Top/Left: Type A name + 3-4 defining traits
- Bottom/Right: Type B name + 3-4 defining traits
- Focus on how the internal motivation differs even when surface behavior looks similar
- Classic pairs: 2 vs 9, 3 vs 1, 4 vs 6, 5 vs 9, 6 vs 4 (counterphobic vs Four), 7 vs 3, 8 vs 1, 9 vs 5

### Healthy vs Unhealthy
- Left: "Type 3 at their best"
- Right: "Type 3 under pressure"
- Reference Riso & Hudson health levels

### Wings
- Left: "4w3 — more outward, status-aware"
- Right: "4w5 — more inward, philosophical"

### Instincts (3-panel)
- SP | SX | SO of one type
- Use Chestnut's subtype work as the anchor

## Visual formula
- 50/50 split (or 33/33/33 for 3-panel)
- Each side: distinct dominant color (e.g., cool blue vs warm rust)
- Matching subject silhouettes or symbolic objects
- Typography consistent across sides (same font, different color)

## Copy-side strategy
- Hook = the confusion itself ("Are you a 2 or a 9?")
- Body = the differentiator (internal motivation)
- Citation = ideally one that addresses misidentification
- CTA = "Take the full test to know for sure"

## Common mistakes
- ❌ Making one side "the good one" (stereotyping)
- ❌ Traits too similar (must be differentiating)
- ❌ Cluttering with too many bullets (3-4 per side max)
- ❌ Omitting the "this is a simplification" footer

## Hedging
- Always include: "A simplification — take the test for your real profile"
- Never claim reading a 3-panel image confirms your type
