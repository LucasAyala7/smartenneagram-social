# M7 — Celebrity Type Deconstruction

## When to use
- Pillar A (Signs & Identification — via famous examples)
- Pillar C (Type in Context — celebrity's public life)
- IG Feed (carousel works best)
- High-engagement format (tagged fans engage strongly)
- HIGH COMPLIANCE RISK — see hedging rules

## Format
- Carousel, 6-8 slides
- Slide 1 = "Why [Celebrity] reads as Type X"
- Slides 2-N = 1 trait + 1 public-evidence example + 1 theory citation
- Final slide = disclaimer + CTA

## Celebrity selection rules
- **OK**: public figures with well-documented interviews, autobiography, body of work
- **AVOID**: minors, recently deceased within 6 months (unless tastefully done), anyone embroiled in active scandal, private citizens
- **USE existing site content**: the site has 33 celebrities documented in `/enneagram-celebrities/` — prefer those for consistency
- **PREFER**: figures across gender, race, geography to diversify the content

## Hedging — ABSOLUTE rules

Never:
- "Celebrity IS Type X"
- "We typed Celebrity as..."
- "Clearly Celebrity is..."

Always:
- "Celebrity READS AS Type X"
- "Celebrity EXHIBITS BEHAVIORS CONSISTENT WITH Type X"
- "Based on public interviews, Celebrity APPEARS TO RESONATE with..."

## Required disclaimer (last slide + end of caption)
> *Typing public figures is inherently speculative. This reflects publicly available material and common community consensus — not a professional assessment.*

## Visual formula
- NO photorealistic rendering of the celebrity's face (likeness/rights risk)
- Use SILHOUETTE, stylized illustration, or abstract symbolic representation
- Background colors keyed to their "era" or signature aesthetic
- Trait slides use iconography + typography, not photos

## Image prompt guidance
- **NEVER** generate "a photorealistic portrait of [celebrity]"
- Instead: "Stylized abstract silhouette of a person [in signature pose], [signature era color palette]"
- Or: "Symbolic objects associated with [celebrity's work] — [list objects]"
- Or: typography-heavy slides with minimal imagery

## Copy-side strategy
- Hook = "[Celebrity] reads as Type [X]. Here's the evidence."
- Body = summary of 2-3 strongest trait matches
- Citation block from a theory author
- Reflection = invite debate ("Agree? What's your typing?")
- CTA = "Take the test and see how YOUR type maps to celebrities you admire"
- **Include disclaimer in caption**

## Common mistakes
- ❌ Rendering a realistic face (legal + aesthetic)
- ❌ Absolute statements ("is", "definitely")
- ❌ Skipping the disclaimer
- ❌ Picking a celebrity in active crisis (brand-risk)
- ❌ Typing based on rumors vs documented material
