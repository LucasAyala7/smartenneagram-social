# M1 — Photo + Brand + Headline

## When to use
- Pillar A (Signs & Identification)
- Pillar C (Type in Context)
- Trigger: identification, curiosity
- Best for IG Feed (not LinkedIn — too visual-pop there)

## Visual formula
- Single cinematic photograph of a person, embodying an emotional state
- Brand logo (top-right, 50px)
- Gradient overlay (bottom 40%, black-to-transparent)
- Short headline in white over the gradient, bottom-left
- No subhead — the caption does the work

## Headline discipline
- 3-7 words maximum
- Pattern: *"[Noun/phrase] + [stakes/specificity]"*
- Examples:
  - "Reasons you feel empty at parties"
  - "When 'I'm fine' is a lie"
  - "The loneliest people in the room"
  - "Why your ex keeps being a Four"
  - "Things Sixes notice first"
- Starts with an article or plural when possible (scroll-grabber)

## Image prompt guidance
- Describe ONE subject doing ONE specific action
- Include: camera body + lens + aperture (commit to one)
- Lighting: natural, window, golden hour, side-light — avoid flat overhead
- Leave visual breathing room bottom-left for headline overlay
- Skin texture realistic, not smoothed
- Muted palette, not Instagram-saturated
- Mood matches headline exactly (if "empty at parties", show party with subject slightly apart)

## Copy-side strategy
- The caption starts with the SAME phrase as the headline, then expands
- The image gives the emotion; the caption gives the evidence (citation)
- Hook caption line should deepen the image's mood, not repeat it

## Common mistakes
- ❌ Overloaded headline (10+ words) — the image does too much
- ❌ Subject smiling generically — kills the emotional specificity
- ❌ Headline that states the type — "Reasons Fours feel empty" spoils the reveal
- ❌ Using stock photo (flat lighting gives it away)

## Hedging
- If the post identifies someone as a type, keep it CONDITIONAL in the headline ("When you feel..." not "If you're a Four...")
